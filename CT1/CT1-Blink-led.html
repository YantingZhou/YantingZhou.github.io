PARA-PORTRAIT

Time: Sep. 2021
Course: Creative Technology
Instructor: Maxim Safioulline

Experiment with creating different patterns of blinking LED. You can use Morse code, you can try to recreate a musical rhythm, or any other familiar patters. Use buttons and potentiometers to modify and control the execution of your code. Create 3 of those rhythmic patterns and capture their execution and modification on video for documentation.

First I started with a single led, I used 10 olm resistor.

Then I tried to add a button as a switch and a potentiometer controlling brightness or blinking speed.

I wanted to adjust the brightness or delay with the potentiometer, but I failed because I found digitalWrite() can only export 0 or 1 value. I searched for solution but Google told me that ESP32 doesn't support "analogWrite".

Sample text. Click to select the text box. Click again or double click to start editing the text.

The outcome has changeable patterns. When button not pressed, the LED is breathing pattern. Then if button pressed, the LED begin to blink, with a potentiometer at right that can adjust the frequency of the blinking.
