RISE OF THE BABY SHARK

Time: Oct. 2021
Course: Creative Technology 1
Instructor: Maxim Saffioulline

Create an interactive system (a game, a story, a performance, etc) that relies on the recognition and tracking of the real-world objects by a machine learning system (ML5js). Explore the possibilities of the technology: what it can and cannot do? how subtle can the variations be? what is easy and what is hard to do with the existing tech? Starting with your big idea outline a backup plan - what is plan B to scale it down? What is plan C? What will work if nothing else works?

Formulate the proposal for an interactive experience controlled by real-world things not connected to your computer in any way - household objects, your body, etc. Build your first prototype using P5js with ML5js library.

Tools: P5js, PosNet

DESIGN PROCESS

Research & Experiment

At the beginning I downloaded various apps using LIDAR to experiment with different inputs and outputs. There are 3Dscanner using rear camera to create low poly model and ScandyPro using front camera to create delicate model. After comparing and analyzing I chose the latter for its intimacy.

I created large amount of models using my face, hands and other body parts and also tried to render them in different modes like monochrome, wireframes and normals.

In the earlier stage, I wanted to build a maze. I created several shapes to represent the wall. I found that the circle is more suitable for the wall because I can use dist() to cover the range of circle within a line of code. However, the rectangle has four walls, that means I have to write four line of long code to define its range for each.

I want to make obstacles move, now my circles are independent fixed values, its hard to met every of them one by one, so I decided to use array.
