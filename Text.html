TEXTS

Time: Nov. 2021
Course: Dev Studio 2
Instructor:  Jennifer Schanen Rider, Sean Donahue  

Brief
 Writing systems and their articulation sit at the intersection of technology, language, and culture. The ability to reproduce these systems is integral to deeply-rooted cultural notions regarding memory, power, history, the nation-state, personal identity, media and definitions of authority and knowledge. 

This course will move beyond the concerns about typographic style that dominate most text-based design discussions and instead explore the design of texts as the visual manifestation of these intersections between technology, language, and culture and the staging of these systems as mechanisms that engage with and interact with people. We will look at how the design of tools for reading and writing influence what can be said, how it can be said, and by whom, and there by, act as agents in issues of power, access and authority. Students will examine the materiality of visible language and explore the ways in which a text’s embodiment can influence its rhetorical, structural, and experiential dimensions.

DESIGN PROCESS

Part 1: Media Translation

Step 1. Select a text

Select a text that has personal significance to you. This could be something that informed your trajectory or speaks to your interests moving forward. You may select a text from any of your class readings. 

From a single letter to a mysterious symbolic language, graphic designer Saki Mafundikwa presents a variety of writing scripts spanning the African continent, highlighting the historical and cultural heritage implications of these scripts and symbols, and urging African designers to draw new inspiration from these graphic scripts. The full presentation could be summarized in his favorite Ghanaian script, Sankofa, which means "return and gain", or "learn from the past".

The reason that I chose this article is that I really appreciate what Saki said: Looking inward.
I think this is an attitude that not only African designer but also designers from all over the world should take, to see through the inside of their own culture, seeking an inherent power to fight for decolonizing design.

Full Text

Step 2. Select 3 Medium

Select two of the following media and you will be assigned a third. 

  • Social media platform (Twitter)
  • Animated GIF; motion graphic
  • Voice to text

Step 3. Experiment with formats

Experiment with formatting your text in each medium. How does the form and context alter the flow of the writing? How would you define your format, technology, medium, audience, habits of reading, and genre expectations? Who is the reader you imagine?

Iterations

Part 2: Mediated Text/s

Compose a text-based response to your original text. Share your point-of-view. What is the dialogue between the two texts?  

1. Select 3 starting points that you’re interested in exploring.

2. Sketch the relationship between your two texts.
What is the sequence of the read? Is one annotating the other? Are they in agreement / disagreement? How do the voices connect? Intersect? Map the connections. What is their relationship?

 3. What opportunities does each medium afford?  Does your choice of medium extend / counter/ emphasize your argument?

3 Voices in My Work

1st is the original text, I highlighted the phrases of classification of the alphabet. They are waving and dynamic, because I see them as my source of inspiration. I wanted to make the pictograph as the center of the fluctuation to made it more clear. 

 2nd is my response with iterations. I started with my association of my own culture, where there is a visual similarity between the pictograph and Chinese knot. I made the infographic of the two with my research of both, constructing it with my findings of different characteristics. 

 3rd is Floating in the air is disassembled and reassembled them into my own interpretation of the overall texts together. It is another layer of response to the original text and my previous response. It is made in virtual reality because its fictional and not pre-existed.
Then is the bg, displaying in the museum itself is an indication of colonial context, referring to how the African culture is exhibited in themed-museum. I choose the white box - contemporary art gallery to put in the new totem, to imply the historical position of the culture but in a contemporary context. African and Asian culture are both ancient cultures but suffering from Racism and Orientalism.
Two voices communicating with each other just by standing together and facing each other, and generating new meaning and symbol in a general context. 

FINAL OUTPUT

A Projected Extention of Reality

The final product is projected as an extension of reality, and the folding of the projection allows the reader to move back and forth between the original text and my response. The three motions echo the relationship between each other.
