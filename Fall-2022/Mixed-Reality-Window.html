FLORA YANTING ZHOU

Mixed Reality Window Chef

Time: Dec. 2022
Course: Concept Studio 2
Instructor: Jenny Rodenhouse, Ben Hooker
Tool: Blender, Adobe Premire

EVERYTHING AS INPUT


Computer vision systems are actively transforming our visual field into new forms of machine sensing and controlling, turning everything within its field of view into an input. 

As environments are increasingly observed by autonomous cameras, there is an algorithmic point of view that we can learn to design and interact with. How can computer vision inform our practice as designers? How can designers, who work on behalf of the people, influence computer vision, augmented reality, and this technological value system as it expands into the social sphere?

Final Project Brief

Windows are important evaluations when people choose whether their home, workplace, or even short stays. They imply the larger environment where people live and states of living. 

What if you and the space you are in, together, are placed inside an augmented skybox? 

Historically, framed objects tend to trigger the interaction of being gazed and appreciated. Such as paintings in gallery and colored glass in the chapel. Windows are the most common frames seen in private spaces. What if they are perceived as canvas? Different places reflect complex characteristic. How do windows relates to this? 

In my prototype, computer vision alter the window view base on its own understanding with the imagination of AI, providing a generative window recipe. This project is about how computer vision sees us and our environment. I want to explore the term “Immersive” in a psychological aspect, and find a balance between a complete virtual experience versus an augmented environment, which is more related to people’s private space.
