FLORA YANTING ZHOU

My humidity - Data Visualization

Time: March. 2022
Course: Creative Technology 2
Instructor: Maxim Safioulline
Tool: HTML, P5js, Arduino, ESP32, Light sensor, Temperature sensor, Tilt sensor

Project Brief:

THE MEDIATED EXTENSION OF SELF

For this project we are going to explore how digital technolgy extends and augments our senses, our bodies and our visions of ourselves. We'll start by creating devices that would allow us to collect data from our immediate surroundings; that would serve as extensions of our bodies and sensory organs. We will organize and distill this data to a meaningful set and explore ways of presenting it that could uncover possible underlying patterns.

DESIGN PROCESS

Building a Data-collection Device

  • A light sensor to detect the light environment I am in;
  • A tilt sensor to calculate the frequency of my water drinking;
  • A temperature sensor in the box to record the water temperature in my cup.

Collecting My Drinking Data

  • I kept recording my drinking habbit for a week:
  • In the mording I used to drink a cup of hot coffee and sometimes in the afternoon I had iced beverage.
  • The light, frequency and tempreature data collected was sent to Adafruit IO Cloud whenever the device is powered.

Visualizing my Drinking Habbit

  • The smallest dark blue dots mean the number of times water was drunk. Some slightly larger dots can be seen representing once drink in this minute.
  • The medium sized greenish circle represents the temperature in the cup. The larger the circle, the higher the temperature of the water in the cup at the moment, and vice versa.
  • The largest background circle represents the light environment. The brighter the color, the more intense the light is.

Insights gained from the project

The project transforms the data collected in real space into virtual images. I feel that the small device I designed is like a filter to extract signals from the noise of my life. In a sense, the body's movements in 3D space are translated into numbers, as if the self is abstracted.
