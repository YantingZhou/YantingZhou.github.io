FLORA YANTING ZHOU

Mirror of image

Time: Apr. 2022
Course: Dev Studio 4 Interactions
Instructor: Elise Co, Sean Donahue
Tool: Spark AR, Photoshop

Project Brief:
This module explores two threads. The first is the use of “technology” to create, foster, enable and facilitate interaction. The second is the exchanges that result from or are instigated by these productions—engagements, happenings, provocations, experiences. These two threads are commingled to offer new models for understanding and more importantly exploring interaction through making/prototyping.

We will consider what “technology” is; explore ideas of prototyping in and with publics; and critically reflect on the resulting implications. Emphasis will be placed on what is being instigated and considered by the design propositions each student puts forward for an individual, community or public to engage with. 

DESIGN PROCESS

Part 1: Field Observation

ASSIGNMENT 1
In-class OBSERVATION - Working in pairs, find a public, shared digital OR physical place/space and observe and document the interactions present there.

  • The public space we chose was Discord, a platform with variety of servers of multiple topics. 
  • Discord is used in many ways, from gaming to education.

ASSIGNMENT 2
Continue your OBSERVATION with your partner, synchronously and asynchronously if possible, and have your “raw” observations and insights ready to discuss Friday. Group, and consider, your interactions as
- Person to person
- Person to technology
- Technology to technology

  • I researched some of the features and functions of the Discord platform, mainly how users interact with the technology.

ASSIGNMENT 4 / SELF DOCUMENTATION

For 48 hours, use the same framework of documenting human/human, human/tech and tech/tech interactions, with YOURSELF as the primary human subject. Your “site” will be variable, and will be wherever you are located, plus whatever digital spaces you visit.

From your full set of documentation, Identify and specifically define three interactions by revising/refining your documentation with a specific lens/intent; 

  • From two of my 15 min documentation, I extracted the momentwhen I see there is an age-restricted channel that asked if I want to continue to see the NSFW page.
  • It's interested that this page didn't block me, but it raise my curiosity in this server instead.

Part 2: Design Intervention

ASSIGNMENT 5
Design, deploy and document an intervention that explores an issue or question from your work/site this week.  

  • For my intervention, I built a server and invite a moderation robot, I kept sending images to test if the image will be moderated.
  • The recognition is not particularly accurate, but I noticed that images identified as NSFW content are moderated within seconds of upload.

ASSIGNMENT 6
Identify the most interesting “moments” from your intervention deployment.
For each moment, list what was interesting about it. What issues came up, what questions were raised?
Select one issue/question and design another intervention that interrogates that area. Plan to deploy next week. Your proposal should include text description as well as graphical representation / mockups. Also include your best-guess speculation about possible outcomes of the deployment. What might happen?

  • From my last convention, I chose the moment when the user sees the image for a few seconds and then disappears, and according to this moment I listed many proposals.
  • The main focus is on how the images are moderated and modified.

ASSIGNMENT 7 / PIVOT
Create your own slides:
A statement of design intent, including “that moment when…” you want to GENERATE
Graphical representation of your proposal for a designed intervention utilizing digital/electronic technology, including how you imagine it to work (storyboard)
Supporting documentation of moments and insights from your observation

  • 1.0 Creating the moment when image convey the facial expression of viewer.
    when images afford the complexity of users’ reaction and emotion;

  • 2.0 Creating the moment when people can see their own reaction along with images.
    when images function as a digital mirror;

  • I am redesigning the role of image, visualizing the participation of receiver to rebuild user’s emotional interaction with a photo (whether negative or positive)

Final Work & Presentation Slide

Discription

  • The Mirror of Image is a simulated social media feed page that will take users’ facial reactions and project them onto the image they see. 
  • By randomizing the images, I intended to show the uncertainty of information feed on social media. The uniqueness of each set of the image provides different contexts and expectations.
  • The interaction happens after people scroll down the page. The application will allow people to see the shocking image for a few seconds before the camera is called and freezes their facial expressions.

Observation

  • People have multiple emotional reactions, some find it amusing, others feel surveillance. 
  • The same thing is that it makes the already shocking images even more shocking.
  • Shocking pictures followed by people’s own face would somehow make people relate the pictures to themself psychologically, reinforcing the reaction.
