FLORA YANTING ZHOU

Enter My Foodie Dream!

Foodie Dreamspace

Time: March. 2022
Course: Creative Technology 2
Instructor: Maxim Safioulline
Tool: Blender, Mozilla Hub

Project Brief:

DIGITAL DREAM SPACE

With this project we'll begin the exploration of virtual environments. We'll look at how digital spaces can emulate or build upon the physical realm, and we'll take on the spaces that are not possible or not necessary in the material world, but present us with some exciting creative possibilities in the digital medium.

The outcome of this project will be an environment you can call your digital home on the Web. Consider not only the elements that you would like to include but also the kinds of activities that your environment would be suitable for.

DESIGN PROCESS

Sketching the Dream

  • I wanted to create a dream world full of favorite foods, in which the food was magnified into a landscape.

Sketching the Dream

  • Extruded cylinders to make bowls.
  • Extrude the rectangular body to make the basic shape platform of noodles, then subdivide the surface, and select the side of the surface at intervals to move vertically to make the shape of noodles.
  • Use Ico Sphere to make "fruits" and move some points to make it irregular. Select shade smooth.
  • Steamed buns and steamers are modified with cylinders.
  • Rotated the surface of the bun to simulate the effect of shrinking pleats.
  • The separation of the fondue pot is made using the Bezier curve
  • The water inside is the same as the soup of noodles, which is plane.
  • Dumpling added mirror modifier. 
  • Originally a rectangle, split the surface and moved the edges to sculpt it.
  • Finally, the combination. 
  • My idea is to use the noodles as the main path of the scene.

Animating the Scene

  • I originally used Blender's own wave modifier to create the soup effect, but this animation has no keyframes and cannot be exported and saved to a GLTF file. So I changed it to a rotating animation.
  • A similar situation occurred with the hot pot soup.
  • The other animations are also some basic rotation, scaling and movement.
  • The total length of the animation is 4 seconds. Some of the objects have a 2 second loop and some are 4 seconds, in order to make the animation more vivid and rhythmic.

Coloring the Dream

  • To get more detail, I searched for tutorials on how to do texture paint. 
  • I painted the texture of the greens, the buns and the plates. I didn't use real mapping but solid colors because I wanted to make the scene look more dreamy.

Coloring the Dream

  • I exported the entire scene as one GLTF file with animation and texture information and uploaded it to Mozilla Hub.
  • The model is walkable so the user in the room can interact with my scene.
  • I also exported some smaller food items separately and added them to the scene as interactable models.

  https://hubs.mozilla.com/Ufzu2e6/ethical-hungry-sphere

Insights gained from the project

The creation of Dreamspace made me more interested in the creation of virtual spaces. The class interacted with my classmates by entering each other's space, and each person's different style of scenes had a special way of interaction.
