Hyper PsychoGeo

Time: Oct. 2021
Instructor: Tim Durfee

Pick an object or a space that either has direct personal significance for you, or about which you have (or can acquire) a lot of knowledge (or opinion). 
Capture or reproduce this object or space. This could be a photograph (where the non-relevant parts are removed), or a collage of photos, or a 3D model, or a digital or hand drawing. 

DESIGN PROCESS

Map a Neighborhood

When hitting the concept of Memory Plaza and Psychogeography, or to map something, my intuition is to map my childhood, so straightforward. It is perhaps simple and common seen to make map of neighborhood, but it is meaningful to myself more than ever. Those memories flushed, there is no other circumstances more fluent than this time when I strarted to design something.

I used to live in a big community that all of the colleague of my parents live together, a place that almost everyone know each other. And it was sophisticated like having its own system - canteen, grocery, hostel - too much to explore.

All over my primary school years I got to know lots of interesting friend. We spend so much cherrished time together after school, playing like partner in crime. I'd never forget when we set fire on rooftop and race bicycles over speed rushing into bushes.

So many significant points of memory, including my first impression about death: my rabbit died for jumping off window,or the young solder died in car accident in front of the road.

I also drew the history of the place, mixing together with my personal experiences. It seem that time and space was ambiguous in the abstract of the pencil lines...
