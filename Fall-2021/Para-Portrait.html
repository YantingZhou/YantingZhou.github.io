FLORA YANTING ZHOU

PARA-PORTRAIT

Time: Sep. 2021
Instructor: Tim Durfee
Tool: LiDAR, Photoshop  

Brief
Create a self-portrait using 3D (Record3D, 3Dscanner) requires phone with LIDAR. Carefully research how it creates images that is unique from the others - optically, mechanically, chemically, and/or digitally.  Experiment with this tool, focusing on the qualities, glitches, capabilities specifically related to its design. As particular qualities emerge from this research, continue your experimentation to develop your own techniques that reveal or expose (or mis-use, or exploit, or amplify) the inherent nature of your tool. 

DESIGN PROCESS

Research & Experiment

At the beginning I downloaded various apps using LIDAR to experiment with different inputs and outputs. There are 3Dscanner using rear camera to create low poly model and ScandyPro using front camera to create delicate model. After comparing and analyzing I chose the latter for its intimacy.

I created large amount of models using my face, hands and other body parts and also tried to render them in different modes like monochrome, wireframes and normals.

Sample Title

Sample Text

3D scans records more aspect of the space and objects. When placing myself into larger scenes, it’s like the model caught a vibe, an atmosphere that me once exist “here” in a place.

By setting closer range, a scan could be more detailed, it distract people/object out from its world. Glitches generated from displacement, which is also a product of time. These disordered meshes are not meaningless, they creates from objects, and the imperfection records the movements like how the lights changed and how the limbs are slightly moved.

Portrait Making

To make the final work I tried several version of collage.

First I tried to put all the elements into one canvas follow my instict. However it turned out to be too fancy.

Then I tired to reduce the elements, and making the picture narrative.

One of my favourite versions. I tried to stress the accidental explosive pattern which indicates the uniqueness of the tool.

FINAL WORK

At end of the project I chose this simple and bold version. With the strong contrast of B&W it hit the audience immediately. 

This is my first work in MDP. Under Tim's guidance, I learned that the final presentation of a work is not necessarily a pile of elements, that "more" does not necessarily mean a high degree of completion, and that "less" can sometimes be a powerful blow. What matters is the process and research of the work, and the message it conveys.
